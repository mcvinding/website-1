## What is EEG101?

Electroencephalography (EEG), discovered exactly 100 years ago, is the most widely used functional brain imaging modality worldwide, contributing to over 5,000 publications annually. Its popularity stems from its ability to non-invasively measure brain activity with millisecond-level temporal resolution, at relatively low cost. EEG offers profound insights into cognitive and neural processes and has significant potential for societal benefit — from Brain-Computer Interfaces for motor rehabilitation to diagnostic tools in neurology.

EEG101 is a **COST Action network** (CA24148) launched in 2025, exactly 101 years after Hans Berger's discovery of EEG. We bring together researchers from across Europe and beyond to address the fragmentation that limits collaboration, data sharing, and reproducibility in EEG research.

## The challenge

Despite widespread use and significant potential, fundamental EEG research faces substantial challenges:

**Fragmentation in data and methodology**
Variability across laboratories and the absence of standardised protocols leads to inconsistencies, making it difficult to replicate or compare findings. This fragmentation hinders cross-laboratory collaborations and meta-analyses.

**Limited data sharing and accessibility**
Despite the vast amount of EEG data generated, much of it remains siloed. Open FAIR-compliant sharing is rare, stifling collaboration and preventing large-scale meta-analyses.

**Lack of standardised tools and workflows**
The field lacks widely adopted, open-source tools and workflows for EEG data analysis. Many laboratories rely on custom-built solutions, limiting reproducibility.

**Unequal access to resources and training**
Research capacity varies significantly across Europe, with many laboratories — especially in Inclusiveness Target Countries (ITCs) — facing limited access to resources and advanced training.

**Barriers to interdisciplinary collaboration**
EEG research spans neuroscience, psychology, engineering, and clinical sciences. Structured opportunities for interdisciplinary collaboration are limited.

## Our response

EEG101 addresses these challenges through four interconnected approaches:

1. **Curated Data Repository** — A meta-directory of standardised, FAIR-compliant EEG datasets, overcoming fragmentation in data access and enabling large-scale analyses.

2. **Open Science Materials** — Working Groups develop and distribute open-source tools, pre-registration templates, standardised workflows, and reporting guidelines.

3. **Training Resources** — Extensive training materials, Summer Schools, podcasts, and asynchronous online resources, prioritising Young Researchers & Innovators and those from ITCs.

4. **Networking** — Short-Term Scientific Missions, Virtual Mobility Grants, and a distributed conference model connect researchers across disciplines and countries.

## Our scientific pillars

EEG101 works across three interconnected pillars:

| Pillar | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| **Standardise** | Standardised reporting guidelines, pre-registration templates, and analytical workflows |
| **Integrate** | Centralised platform, FAIR datasets, interoperable pipelines, and normative databases |
| **Advance** | Innovation through STSMs, VMGs, Summer Schools, podcast, and distributed conferences |

## Impact

**Short-term (Years 1–2):** Standardised protocols and tools; improved training access for YRIs and ITC researchers; new analytical tools in collaboration with software developers.

**Medium-term (Years 3–4):** Larger pool of comparable data enabling more robust findings; empowered next-generation scientists; enhanced multicentre clinical studies.

**Long-term (Beyond Year 4):** A lasting culture of open, rigorous, collaborative EEG research; EEG applications in early detection and diagnosis; contributions to the global neurotechnology market projected to reach USD$38 billion by 2032.

## Inclusivity and diversity

EEG101 is committed to equity across all its activities. A dedicated **Inclusivity Monitoring Committee** tracks engagement and develops strategies for supporting underrepresented groups. We provide ring-fenced funding tracks for Young Researchers & Innovators and ITC-based researchers, and Virtual Mobility Grants for those unable to travel.

## Building on existing efforts

EEG101 builds on and connects established European initiatives including [#EEGManyLabs](https://eegmanylabs.com) (>230 labs, 32 countries), [CuttingEEG](https://cuttingeeg.org), [ARTEM-IS](https://artem-is.org), and [FieldTrip](https://fieldtriptoolbox.org) — creating a network capable of addressing challenges that no single initiative can tackle alone.

## The COST framework

COST (European Cooperation in Science and Technology) is a funding agency for research and innovation networks. EEG101 (CA24148) is funded through the Horizon Europe programme of the European Union, running from 2025 to 2029.
